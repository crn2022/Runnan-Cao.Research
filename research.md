### 1. A Computational Framework for Object Recognition
*How does the brain transition from seeing "features" to recognizing "people"?*

We have identified a hierarchical transition in the human temporal lobe. While the Ventral Temporal Cortex (VTC) employs an **axis-based code** (analyzing features like texture or shape), the Medial Temporal Lobe (MTL) utilizes a **region-based code** to form invariant representations.

### 2. Social Cognition & Neural Dynamics
*How do we form first impressions and infer intentions?*

Beyond identity, faces convey rich social information. I investigate how these computations adapt across different contexts, such as emotion recognition and trait judgment.

### 3. Clinical Applications: Autism & Schizophrenia
*Bridging the gap between neural circuits and social deficits.*

I am currently extending my computational framework to understand atypical face processing in Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Schizophrenia. By comparing intracranial recordings and fMRI data, we aim to pinpoint the specific circuit disruptions that lead to altered social trait judgments.
